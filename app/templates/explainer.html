üìä Expected Value: The Holy Grail for Stoners Who Can Math

EV is what separates pros from the schmucks tailing Instagram handicappers. It's the mathematical calculation of whether a bet is actually +money in the long run.

The formula is simple, even if you're three bong rips deep:

EV = (Probability of Winning √ó Potential Profit) - (Probability of Losing √ó Stake)

When a sportsbook offers odds, they're telling you what they think the probability is, plus their juice (the vig). When our calculated probability is different from the implied odds, that's where your edge lives.

EV of 10% means if you bet $100, you'll theoretically make $10 in the long run. It's like the house edge at casinos, but in reverse - you've got the advantage, you beautiful nerd.
